--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SITE INFORMATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Site Name: Toyopán Sacred Forest Core (The Shrine)
Location: Reserva Huaravito, Piedades Sur, San Ramón, Alajuela, Costa Rica
Coordinates: 10.1065°N, 84.5666°W
Elevation: 1,250 m.a.s.l.
Forest Type: Premontane humid forest (Holdridge)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TEMPORAL COVERAGE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Start Date: January 2022
End Date: December 2025
Duration: 48 months (4 years)
Temporal Resolution: Monthly means

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
MEASUREMENT PROTOCOL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sensor Type: Digital temperature sensor (specific model not documented)
Sensor Height: 1.5 m above ground (standardized)
Sensor Location: Fixed position within forest core sector (The Shrine)
Measurement Frequency: Continuous (aggregated to monthly means)
Calibration: Not formally documented (LIMITATION)

Regional Reference: San Ramón area meteorological data (approx. 10 km distance)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DATA FIELDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. year
   Description: Year of measurement
   Format: YYYY (integer)
   Units: N/A
   Example: 2022

2. month
   Description: Month of measurement
   Format: 1-12 (integer)
   Units: N/A
   Example: 1 (January)

3. season
   Description: Climatic season classification
   Format: Text (Dry or Wet)
   Values: 
     - Dry: December, January, February, March, April
     - Wet: May, June, July, August, September, October, November
   Notes: Based on regional precipitation patterns

4. forest_core_temp_C
   Description: Mean monthly temperature at forest core sensor
   Format: Decimal
   Units: Degrees Celsius (°C)
   Precision: ±0.1°C
   Missing Data: None

5. regional_ambient_temp_C
   Description: Reference temperature from San Ramón area
   Format: Decimal
   Units: Degrees Celsius (°C)
   Source: Regional meteorological station data
   Notes: Approximate 10 km from site; serves as matrix baseline

6. delta_T_C
   Description: Temperature differential (biogenic cooling effect)
   Formula: forest_core_temp_C - regional_ambient_temp_C
   Format: Decimal
   Units: Degrees Celsius (°C)
   Interpretation: Negative values indicate forest is cooler than regional ambient

7. notes
   Description: Qualitative observations or special conditions
   Format: Text
   Examples: "El Niño influence", "Cooler wet season", "Baseline year initiation"

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
KEY RESULTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Mean Annual Temperature (Forest Core): 19.5°C
Interannual Standard Deviation (σ): ~0.2°C
Mean Biogenic Cooling Effect (ΔT): -3.7°C
Annual Thermal Amplitude: 2.1°C (18.8°C min to 20.9°C max)

Dry Season Mean: 20.0°C
Wet Season Mean: 19.3°C
Seasonal Amplitude: 0.7°C

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
LIMITATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Single sensor: No spatial replication within forest or multiple heights
2. Calibration not formally documented
3. Instrument precision unknown (estimated ±0.5°C based on typical consumer sensors)
4. Regional reference station not co-located; introduces spatial variability
5. No concurrent humidity, wind speed, or solar radiation measurements

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DATA QUALITY STATEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This dataset represents four years of continuous monitoring with consistent 
methodology. While instrument-level precision is not formally characterized, 
the temporal stability (σ ~0.2°C across years) suggests robust internal 
consistency. The dataset is suitable for:
  - Interannual trend analysis
  - Seasonal decomposition
  - Long-term monitoring baseline establishment

The dataset requires spatial replication (distributed sensor network) to 
validate horizontal and vertical temperature gradients.
