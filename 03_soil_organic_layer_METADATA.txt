SITE INFORMATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Site: Permanent Plot LTEMS-P001 (30 × 30 m)
Location: Toyopán Sacred Forest Core, Reserva Huaravito
Coordinates: 10.1065°N, 84.5666°W
Elevation: ~1,250 m.a.s.l.
Forest Type: Premontane humid forest

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
MEASUREMENT PROTOCOL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sampling Design: 10 permanent points distributed across plot
Marker Type: Subsurface metal washers (5 cm depth) + GPS coordinates
Re-measurement Frequency: Annual (February, dry season)

Measurement Method:
  1. Locate marked point using GPS and plot coordinates
  2. Insert graduated metal rod vertically until resistance (mineral soil)
  3. Record depth from litter surface to mineral soil contact
  4. Take 3 replicate measurements per point (within 20 cm radius)
  5. Average the 3 replicates
  6. Record qualitative observations (moisture, fungi, roots, disturbance)

Instrument: Graduated metal rod (1 cm increments)
Precision: ±0.5 cm

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DATA FIELDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. point_id
   Description: Unique identifier for each sampling point
   Format: SP## (Soil Point + number)
   Range: SP01 to SP10
   Notes: Permanent markers for re-measurement

2. plot_x_m
   Description: X-coordinate within plot (east-west)
   Format: Decimal
   Units: Meters (m)
   Origin: Southwest corner (0,0)

3. plot_y_m
   Description: Y-coordinate within plot (north-south)
   Format: Decimal
   Units: Meters (m)
   Origin: Southwest corner (0,0)

4. gps_lat
   Description: GPS latitude
   Format: Decimal degrees
   Datum: WGS84
   Precision: ±5 m

5. gps_lon
   Description: GPS longitude
   Format: Decimal degrees
   Datum: WGS84
   Precision: ±5 m

6. year
   Description: Year of measurement
   Format: YYYY
   Example: 2026

7. month
   Description: Month of measurement
   Format: 1-12
   Notes: Standardized to February (dry season) for comparability

8. organic_layer_depth_cm
   Description: Depth of organic layer (litter + humus to mineral soil)
   Format: Decimal
   Units: Centimeters (cm)
   Method: Average of 3 replicate rod insertions
   Precision: ±0.5 cm
   Range: 18.9 - 25.4 cm (this dataset)

9. moisture_status
   Description: Qualitative soil moisture assessment
   Format: Text
   Values: Low, Moderate, High
   Criteria:
     - Low: Dry to touch; litter crumbles easily
     - Moderate: Slightly moist; some cohesion
     - High: Visibly moist; dark color; cohesive

10. surface_condition
    Description: Qualitative description of litter/humus surface
    Format: Text
    Notes: Free-form observations (litter type, root visibility, etc.)

11. fungal_presence
    Description: Visual assessment of fungal activity
    Format: Text
    Values: Low, Moderate, High, Very High
    Indicators: Mycelium visibility, fruiting bodies, decomposition activity

12. notes
    Description: Additional qualitative observations
    Format: Text
    Examples: "White mycelium abundant", "Earthworm activity noted"

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ECOLOGICAL INTERPRETATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The observed mean depth of 21.8 cm (±2.1 cm SD) is characteristic of mature 
tropical premontane forests with long-term litter accumulation and minimal 
disturbance. The high fungal activity indicates active decomposition and 
nutrient cycling.

The spatial pattern shows:
  - Deeper organic layers in central plot (core forest)
  - Shallower layers near plot edges (edge effects)
  - High moisture retention despite dry season sampling (February)

This organic mat contributes to:
  - Soil moisture retention (evaporative cooling mechanism)
  - Thermal insulation (buffering against temperature fluctuations)
  - Grass suppression (physical barrier + allelopathy)
  - Carbon storage

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
LIMITATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Single year baseline (no temporal trend data yet)
2. Small sample size (10 points across 900 m²)
3. Qualitative moisture/fungal assessments (no quantitative gravimetric analysis)
4. No bulk density, carbon content, or nutrient analysis
5. Seasonal bias (dry season only; wet season depths may differ)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RE-MEASUREMENT SCHEDULE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Annual re-measurement every February (dry season standardization)

Metrics to Track Over Time:
  - Organic layer depth trend (stable, increasing, decreasing)
  - Spatial pattern changes
  - Moisture status shifts (climate change indicator)
  - Fungal activity trends

Target: ≥5 years of data by 2031 for trend analysis

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DATA QUALITY STATEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This baseline dataset establishes the current state of organic layer depth 
and surface conditions within the permanent plot. While qualitative assessments 
(moisture, fungal presence) are subjective, the depth measurements are 
standardized and repeatable. The dataset is suitable for:
  - Baseline characterization
  - Long-term trend monitoring
  - Comparative analysis with other forest types

Quantitative soil chemistry and bulk density analysis recommended for future 
detailed carbon storage assessment.
