--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This dataset consolidates biodiversity observations from the Huaravito Reserve 
documented via iNaturalist and cross-referenced with other monitoring platforms 
(Terraware, Restor, NASA GLOBE Observer). It represents opportunistic field 
observations rather than systematic census data.

Primary Platform: iNaturalist Project "Reserva Huaravito / Sitio Natural Sagrado"
URL: https://www.inaturalist.org/projects/reserva-huaravito-sitio-natural-sagrado

As of February 2026:
  Total Observations: 238
  Unique Species: 116
  Temporal Span: 2021-2026 (5 years)

NOTE: This CSV contains a REPRESENTATIVE SAMPLE (25 observations) for 
demonstration purposes. The full dataset of 238 observations is available 
via the iNaturalist project page.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OBSERVATION PROTOCOL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Platform: iNaturalist (primary)
Method: Opportunistic field observations with photographic documentation
GPS Accuracy: ±5-10 m (smartphone GPS)
Taxonomic Verification: Community-based identification via iNaturalist
Quality Grade: Research Grade (when community consensus achieved)

Cross-Platform Integration:
  - Terraware: Forest inventory cross-reference (organizationId=213)
  - Restor: Site profile integration
  - NASA GLOBE Observer: Canopy cover and tree height validation

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DATA FIELDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. obs_id
   Description: Sequential observation number (within this sample dataset)
   Format: Integer
   Range: 1-25 (this sample); full dataset has 238 observations

2. date
   Description: Date of observation
   Format: YYYY-MM-DD
   Precision: Day
   Example: 2021-05-09

3. year
   Description: Year of observation
   Format: YYYY
   Purpose: Facilitates annual summaries

4. species_scientific
   Description: Scientific binomial name
   Format: Genus species
   Authority: Verified via iNaturalist community
   Notes: "sp." indicates genus-level identification only
   Example: Hauya elegans

5. species_common
   Description: Local common name(s)
   Format: Text
   Language: Spanish (Costa Rican vernacular)
   Example: Guayabón, Aguacatillo
   Notes: "NA" indicates no common name documented

6. family
   Description: Taxonomic family
   Format: Text
   Example: Onagraceae, Lauraceae, Orchidaceae

7. growth_form
   Description: Plant growth habit
   Format: Text
   Values: Tree, Shrub, Epiphyte, Vine, Herb
   Example: Tree

8. successional_guild
   Description: Ecological successional role
   Format: Text
   Values: Pioneer, Early-intermediate, Late-successional
   Criteria:
     - Pioneer: Light-demanding, fast-growing, short-lived
     - Early-intermediate: Moderately shade-tolerant
     - Late-successional: Shade-tolerant, slow-growing, long-lived
   Source: Literature-based classification + field observation

9. iucn_status
   Description: IUCN Red List conservation status
   Format: Text
   Values: Endangered, Vulnerable, Near Threatened, Least Concern, Not Listed
   Source: IUCN Red List (https://www.iucnredlist.org)
   Example: Persea schiedeana = Endangered

10. rarity
    Description: Local rarity assessment
    Format: Text
    Values: Common, Uncommon
    Criteria:
      - Common: Frequently observed across multiple habitats
      - Uncommon: Infrequent; limited distribution or specialized habitat
    Notes: Based on field experience within Huaravito and regional knowledge

11. inat_url
    Description: Link to iNaturalist observation
    Format: URL
    Example: https://inaturalist.org/observations/1
    Notes: Placeholder URLs in this sample; actual URLs in full dataset

12. platform_validation
    Description: Which platforms validated this observation
    Format: Text
    Values: iNaturalist, iNaturalist+Terraware, iNaturalist+Restor, etc.
    Notes: Cross-platform presence increases data reliability

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ECOLOGICAL INTERPRETATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The increasing species richness over time (12 → 116 species) reflects:
  1. Expanded spatial coverage of monitoring
  2. Improved observer expertise in field identification
  3. Phenological variation (different seasons capture different taxa)
  4. Community engagement (additional observers contributing)

The dominance of early-intermediate successional species (58%) with significant 
representation of late-successional taxa (23%) indicates a transitional forest 
with characteristics of mature structure despite small fragment size.

The high representation of Hauya elegans (30.3%) is consistent with its 
structural prominence in the western core sector, though this likely reflects 
detectability bias (large, conspicuous trees) rather than absolute numerical 
dominance.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
KEY FINDINGS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Two epiphytic orchid species (Scaphyglottis spp.) documented, indicating 
   primary forest microclimate conditions

2. One IUCN Endangered species (Persea schiedeana) actively monitored

3. Spontaneous recruitment of keystone species (Ficus sp., Annona sp.) beyond 
   baseline 26-species inventory, confirming active zoochorous dispersal

4. 86% of documented species are shade-tolerant or moderately shade-tolerant, 
   consistent with closed-canopy forest

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
LIMITATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Opportunistic sampling (not systematic census)
2. Detectability bias toward large, conspicuous species
3. Spatial bias toward accessible areas (paths, plot boundaries)
4. Seasonal bias (more observations during dry season field visits)
5. Taxonomic bias toward vascular plants (limited fungi, invertebrates)
6. Observer bias (single primary observer; inter-observer variability unknown)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DATA QUALITY STATEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
All observations are georeferenced, timestamped, and photographically documented 
via iNaturalist. Community-based taxonomic verification provides quality control. 
The dataset is suitable for:
  - Species richness trends
  - Phenological monitoring
  - Rare species tracking
  - Multi-year biodiversity trajectory analysis

The dataset is NOT suitable for:
  - Abundance estimation
  - Population density calculation
  - Systematic species accumulation curves

For quantitative abundance data, refer to Tree Census Dataset (02).
